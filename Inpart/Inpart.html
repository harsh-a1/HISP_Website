Inpart Project (Punjab)

The project, a collaboration between University of Oslo and Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education Research (PGIMER), Chandigarh with focus on design of patient centric systems for primary health care) aims at setting up information systems in resource constrained environment. The following activities are being covered as part of this project:

  1. Establishment of a living lab designed in context and in collaboration with end-users to support this development.
  2. HISP India has collaborated extensively to develop systems and trained doctors working on-site on using patient centric systems.
  3. Collaborative research on the challenges and approaches to build such systems. The living lab has provided the site for interaction between the research team and the health functionaries, and presentations have been made on design choices, and challenges and approaches in materializing them.
  4. Build course module on design in both face-to-face and on-line modes.
  5. Development of trained researchers in both institutions capable of carrying high quality research in the area in the future.

Project Implementation
The INTPART project intends to strengthen the primary health care component of health systems by empowering both the stockholders such as Primary/Community Health Workers, Medical Officers as well as the patients. As per Universal Health Coverage Principles, a population database using governments Public Distribution Scheme has been populated at the PHC which is operational as the living lab. The heart of the project lies in building a digital family folder in DHIS2, where households are registered in the system along with its household members creating relationships between the head of the household and its members. These members are then enrolled into different programs in the system given below:

  1. Eligible Couple Register
  2. Maternal Health
  3. Child Health
  4. Tuberculosis Program
  5. Non-Communicable Disease Program
  6. Cause of Death

The Primary/Community Health Workers are the key information collectors and the connecting link between the periphery and the health facilities. They collect the data during their households’ visits and refer patients to the clinic in case the patients need clinical interventions. Since the population is already registered in the system, the doctor at the facility can search the referred/walk-in patient and provide them the required treatment.
Using the above concept, program specific patient registries are being generated, where the patient is then followed-up over the course of the treatment, be it 9 months of Antenatal and Postnatal care, following up a child through Immunisation schedule, managing the TB treatment protocol etc.

Decision Support
The DHIS2 based system has been enhanced by incorporating a basic decision support that shows the latest clinical summary along with vital information from last visit. These enhancements help the clinicians to see the critical data points which are necessary for regular monitoring for providing better care to the patients.

Patient Communication
The system is linked with SMS Gateway APIs for sending regular SMSs to the patients depending on the program they are enrolled in. For example, pregnant women get SMS reminders for their appointments.

Outputs
In addition to default DHIS2 visualisations, the focus has been populating the registers which the Primary/Community Health Workers were maintaining manually till date, the system has been enhanced towards generating the required registers automatically, which also reflected their monthly workplans highlighting the cases which are due for follow-up, thus saving a lot of time in doing these tasks manually.

Future Enhancement
IVRS Integration:  In addition to communication with the patient through SMS, IVRS integration is being studied whereby calls to the patient’s number will be made giving them details about their upcoming visits, health and wellness related information.
